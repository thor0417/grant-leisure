Attractions

The London Eye

Europe's tallest observation wheel and the single most visited attraction in the United Kingdom.

Opposite the Houses of Parliament on the South Bank of the River Thames, the London Eye defines the capital's skyline. Grant Leisure was commissioned by British Airways to provide a feasibility analysis, corporate hospitality and sponsorship program, and operating strategy. Grant Leisure additionally managed both the tender process and selection of an operator. Following the London Eye's success, observation wheels have grown significantly in popularity, many of which are modelled after their predecessor.

Ferrari World

The first branded theme park in the United Arab Emirates, built on an exclusive worldwide rights agreement secured by Grant Leisure.

Grant Leisure was commissioned by Aldar to develop the economic feasibility and concept plan and to secure the worldwide rights for Ferrari World. Boasting over 20 themed attractions and adventures, all under the complex's signature red roof, guests are treated to a taste of being behind the wheel of the world's most prestigious sports car. Ferrari World is a global destination and the defining example of branded leisure development in the Gulf region.

Ocean Park

Hong Kong, China

Grant Leisure redeveloped Ocean Park's master plan and reversed visitor decline through surgical infrastructure recommendations.

Grant Leisure was involved with the redevelopment of Ocean Park's master plan following its first year of operation, presenting recommendations to stem declines in visitor numbers. One notable recommendation connected the bird sanctuary to the Oceanarium with a funicular and improved cable car. That infrastructure decision has heavily contributed to the park's sustained success and visitor growth decade over decade.

Our Dynamic Earth

The largest visitor attraction ever built in Scotland, taking guests on a journey through Earth's past, present, and future.

Dynamic Earth is an edutainment experience addressing issues of population growth, concepts of time, and the realities of climate change. Visitors stand face-to-face with extinct dinosaurs, feel the heat of a tropical rainforest, and fly over glaciers in prehistoric Scotland through a series of interactive exhibits including a 4-D experience. Grant Leisure was retained by the City of Edinburgh to provide the economic feasibility, pre-opening planning, recruitment, training, marketing, and finance for the project.

Legoland

Grant Leisure conducted the complete economic feasibility for the first Legoland in Windsor and guided its expansion into the United States.

Legoland Windsor opened in 1996. Grant Leisure provided management assistance in recruitment, training, and marketing. The original site was an old safari park whose 400 animals Grant Leisure was responsible for safely relocating. Following Windsor's success, Grant Leisure was commissioned to conduct a comparable analysis and site evaluation for Legoland California. Grant Leisure has continued to advise Legoland over the years, making recommendations for management, group sales, and market research.

The Olympic Spirit

Munich, Germany / Toronto, Canada

Grant Leisure held an exclusive worldwide licensing agreement with the International Olympic Committee for a themed edutainment attraction that won the 2005 TEA Award for Outstanding Achievement.

In the early 2000s, Grant Leisure signed an exclusive worldwide licensing agreement with the International Olympic Committee for the development of The Olympic Spirit, a themed visitor attraction and edutainment center. Through computer simulation and innovative staging techniques, visitors experienced the Olympics through interactive sporting activities and friendly competition. The project won the 2005 TEA Award for Outstanding Achievement and the 2005 Innovation Award, presented by the Tourism Federation of Ontario.

Granada Studio Tours

Grant Leisure initiated, conceived, and built Granada Studio Tours from the ground up, serving on the Board of Directors for three years to guarantee results.

The Granada Studio Tour was an entertainment complex based on the ITV programming portfolio in Castlefield, Manchester, which ran from 1988 to 1999. Grant Leisure initiated the project, created the concept, conducted the feasibility study, supervised architecture and construction, and provided all pre-development services. Grant Leisure was appointed to the Board of Directors for three years to guarantee results. The Coronation Street tour proved so successful it was temporarily reopened by popular demand from 2014 to 2015.

Bluewater Shopping Centre

Kent, United Kingdom

The first retail center in the United Kingdom to feature integrated leisure. Today it serves over 42 million guests annually.

Bluewater sits on 240 acres of land in East Kent. The property features 26 acres of lakes, botanical gardens, walking trails, pavilions, and fountains throughout the heart of its retail center. Grant Leisure was commissioned by Shearwater Developments to conduct a feasibility study for the development of leisure elements for the shopping center. Today, Bluewater serves over 42 million guests annually and remains the first retail center in the United Kingdom to feature integrated leisure elements alongside the shopping experience.
