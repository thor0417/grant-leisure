Attractions

The London Eye

Opposite the Houses of Parliament, on the South Bank of the River Thames, stands the London Eye. With its panoramic views, the London Eye is currently Europe's tallest observation wheel and the single most visited attraction in the United Kingdom.

Grant Leisure was commissioned by British Airways to provide a feasibility analysis, corporate hospitality and sponsorship program, and operating strategy -- additionally managing both the tender process and selection of an operator. Following the London Eye's success, observation wheels have significantly grown in popularity, many of which are modeled after their predecessor.

Ferrari World

Grant Leisure was commissioned by Aldar to develop the economic feasibility and concept plan, and secure the worldwide rights for Ferrari World -- the first branded theme park in the United Arab Emirates.

Boasting over 20 themed attractions and adventures, all under the complex's signature red roof, guests are treated to a taste of being behind the wheel of the world's most prestigious sports car. Ferrari World stands as a global destination and a landmark example of branded leisure development in the Gulf region.

Ocean Park

Hong Kong, China

Grant Leisure was involved with the redevelopment of Ocean Park's master plan following its first year of operation -- presenting recommendations to stem visitor decline and unlock the park's full potential.

One such notable recommendation included connecting the bird sanctuary to the Oceanarium with a funicular and improved cable car -- an intervention that has heavily contributed to the park's current success and sustained visitor growth decade over decade.

Our Dynamic Earth

One of the largest visitor attractions ever built in Scotland -- Dynamic Earth takes guests on a journey through Earth's past, present, and future through interactive exhibits utilizing innovative technology, including a 4-D experience.

Grant Leisure was retained by the City of Edinburgh to provide the economic feasibility, pre-opening planning, recruitment, training, marketing, and finance for the attraction. An edutainment experience addressing issues of population growth, concepts of time, and the realities of climate change -- Dynamic Earth stands as Scotland's most ambitious cultural infrastructure project of its era.

Legoland

Grant Leisure conducted the complete economic feasibility for the first Legoland in Windsor, which opened in 1996 -- thereafter providing management assistance in recruitment, training, and marketing.

The original site was an old safari park whose 400 animals Grant Leisure was responsible for safely relocating. Following Legoland Windsor's success, Grant Leisure was commissioned to conduct a comparable analysis and site evaluation for Legoland California. Grant Leisure has continued to advise Legoland over the years, making recommendations for management, group sales, and market research.

The Olympic Spirit

Munich, Germany / Toronto, Canada

Grant Leisure signed an exclusive worldwide licensing agreement with the International Olympic Committee -- developing a themed visitor attraction and edutainment center that brought the Olympic experience to the public.

Through the use of computer simulation and innovative staging techniques, visitors were offered a taste of the Olympics through interactive sporting activities and friendly competition. The Olympic Spirit won a number of prestigious awards -- including the 2005 TEA Award for Outstanding Achievement and the 2005 Innovation Award, presented by the Tourism Federation of Ontario.

Grant Leisure was commissioned to conduct a Feasibility Analysis for the implementation of a significant NBC/Universal intellectual property as both a visitor attraction and traveling exhibition across the United States.

Grant Leisure has a long history of providing consulting services for Universal Studios. Attractions

Granada Studio Tours

Grant Leisure initiated the project, created the concept, conducted the feasibility study, supervised architecture and construction, provided pre-development services, and was appointed to the Board of Directors for 3 years to guarantee results.

Based on the ITV programming portfolio in Castlefield, Manchester, the Granada Studio Tour ran from 1988--1999. The Coronation Street tour of the studios proved so successful it was temporarily reopened by popular demand from 2014 to 2015 -- a testament to the enduring power of the original concept.

Bluewater Shopping Centre

Kent, United Kingdom

Grant Leisure was commissioned by Shearwater Developments to conduct a feasibility study for the development of leisure elements for the shopping center -- pioneering a model that is now the industry standard.

Bluewater sits on 240 acres of land in East Kent. The property features 26 acres of lakes, botanical gardens, walking trails, pavilions, and fountains throughout the heart of its retail center. Today, Bluewater serves over 42 million guests annually and is the first retail center in the United Kingdom to feature integrated leisure elements with the shopping experience.
